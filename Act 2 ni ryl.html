Pork Sinigang Recipe

Description: Pork Sinigang is a classic Filipino sour soup featuring tender pork, fresh vegetables, and a tangy tamarind broth. It's hearty, comforting, and perfect for a family meal. Serves 4-6. Prep time: 15 mins. Cook time: 45 mins.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg (2.2 lbs) pork belly or pork ribs, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 large onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 pack (about 25g) sinigang mix (tamarind seasoning) or 1/2 cup tamarind juice
  • 2-3 cups water or rice wash (for added flavor)
  • 1 large daikon radish (labanos), sliced into rounds
  • 1 eggplant (talong), sliced
  • 1 bunch green beans (sitaw), trimmed
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach) or spinach, leaves separated
  • 2-3 green long chilies (siling haba), optional for mild heat
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a large pot over medium heat. Sauté the onion and tomatoes until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  2. Add the pork pieces and cook until lightly browned on all sides, about 5 minutes.
  3. Pour in the water (or rice wash) and bring to a boil. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until the pork is tender, about 30-40 minutes.
  4. Stir in the sinigang mix (or tamarind juice) and fish sauce. Add the daikon radish and green chilies. Simmer for another 5 minutes.
  5. Add the eggplant and green beans. Cook for 3-5 minutes until vegetables are tender but still crisp.
  6. Finally, add the kangkong leaves and cook for 1-2 minutes until wilted. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Turn off the heat and let it sit for a few minutes. Serve hot with steamed rice.

Tip: For a more authentic flavor, use fresh tamarind instead of the mix—boil tamarind pods in water, strain, and use the juice.
